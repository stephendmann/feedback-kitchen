What Feedback Kitchen doesn't do

The boundaries are the point.

✕

Does not decide grades. You select every grade.

✕

Does not invent comments. Only assembles sentences you wrote.

✕

Does not upload student work to any server.

✕

Does not require a login or institutional account.

✕

Does not replace your LMS. Paste into Moodle, Canvas, Turnitin, or any other system as usual.

✕

Wording assistant never sees identifiers — names and IDs are stripped first.

Everything You Need

Built for lecturers, course coordinators, and tutors who want speed, consistency, and quality feedback.¹

Custom Rubrics

Define your own criteria and weight them however you like. Add rubric descriptors for all four tiers — Excellent, Proficient, Developing, and Unsatisfactory.

Weighted Scoring

Set percentage weights per criterion. The tool automatically calculates weighted scores, applies late penalties, and suggests an overall grade.

Editable Feedback Draft

Each student gets a fully assembled feedback draft — intro, per-criterion commentary, score, late penalty notice, and a grade-tailored closing paragraph. Edit any of it before you send.

One-Click Export

Copy feedback to your clipboard, download a formatted Excel workbook with results, rubric, and feedback templates, or print directly to PDF.

Fully Customisable

Edit every intro and outro paragraph per grade level. Override any score manually. Add personalised comments alongside the auto-generated text.

Multiple Scorers

Build a separate scorer for each course or assessment. Save and switch between them. Export your config as JSON to share with colleagues.

How It Works

From rubric to ready-to-paste feedback in three steps.

1

Build Your Scorer

Use the guided wizard to set up your assessment details, define your criteria and weightings, write rubric descriptors for each tier, and customise the grade feedback text. Takes about 10–20 minutes for a new assessment.

2

Grade a Student

Enter the student's details, select a grade for each criterion from the dropdown, optionally override any score, choose a late penalty if applicable, and watch the overall score and feedback assemble in real time.

3

Export & Move On

Copy the feedback to your clipboard and paste it into Moodle, Turnitin, or your institution's grading system. Download an Excel record of the grading, or print to PDF. Hit "New Student" and repeat.
