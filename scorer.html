Using the DEMO101 / 2026 Short Written Response scorer

This demo scorer helps you mark a short written response using a clear rubric and generate plain‑language feedback. Use it to practise the workflow before building your own scorer.

  • A · Student — Add the student name or identifier and any basic details.
  • B · Rubric — Choose the level that best matches each criterion. The scorer calculates the total from the saved weights.
  • C · Adjustments — Make a small manual adjustment if needed, and note why.
  • D · Feedback — Review or edit the generated feedback so it matches your judgement and tone.
  • E · Notes — Keep any private marker notes here (not shown to the student).

Finish by checking the score, band, and feedback, then use ↺ New Student to mark the next response.

Suggests clearer wording for your feedback — based on your rubric, scores, and optional notes. Never changes marks or grades. You decide what to keep.

What the assistant cannot change

The following parts are fixed and always come from your own marking:

  • Any scores you have entered
  • Any penalties (for lateness, academic integrity, etc.)
  • The greeting (for example, “Hi”)
  • Intro and closing paragraphs (based on the pre-penalty grade)
  • The TOTAL SCORE line
  • Late submission notice and the final penalised score

These stay exactly as generated from your grades, even if you use assistant suggestions. Your professional judgement drives all scores; this tool only helps with wording.

What the assistant uses

To draft or refine wording, the assistant only uses:

  • The rubric descriptors for each criterion
  • The scores you have entered so far
  • (Optional) your private marker’s notes and saved snippets

              
            

          


          
          

            
              
What happens to my data?

            
            

              

                If you use this assistant, the selected text (rubric descriptors, your scores,
                and any notes or snippets) is sent to the AI service to generate a suggestion.
                If you do not use it, your marking stays entirely local to your browser.
              

              

                Built-in protection: student names and IDs entered in Sections A or B
                are automatically removed from anything sent to the AI service, even if they appear
                in Marker’s Notes or your draft. Email addresses are also stripped.
              

              

                Good practice: still use initials or generic references in Marker’s
                Notes where possible — the scrub is a safety net, not a licence to paste PII.
